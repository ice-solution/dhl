Seoul at a Glance
Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is recognized as a leading global city characterized by a thriving economy and a high quality of life. It serves as a significant hub for technology, electronics, design, and business. The city seamlessly blends ancient traditions with modern advancements, showcasing skyscrapers alongside historic palaces and pagodas. Despite its large size, Seoul offers efficient public transportation and accessible dining options.

Currency & Money
The South Korean won (₩ / KRW) is the official currency used throughout Seoul. Seoul is very card-friendly, but it is advisable to carry some cash for markets and street food. ATMs are readily available but typically charge a withdrawal fee of approximately ₩3,000 - ₩5,000 per transaction. Currency exchange services and ATMs can be found at the airport and major shopping malls.​


Power Plugs & Sockets
In South Korea, power plugs and sockets (outlets) of type C and type F are used. The standard voltage is 220V at a frequency of 60Hz. A universal travel adapter is recommended for convenience.​


Seoul Transportation to City:
Seoul's transportation system is designed to be user-friendly and efficient, making it easy for visitors to navigate the city. Here are the key points to consider when using public transportation in Seoul.

Subway: The Seoul subway system is extensive, with key lines connecting central areas and regional lines. It operates 24/7, making it a reliable option for city travel. 

Bus: Buses cover a wide range of destinations in Seoul, including major hotels and districts. They are color-coded for easy identification, and the T-money card is used for boarding and exiting. 

Taxi: Taxis are available 24/7 and can be used for luggage or late-night travel. They are the most expensive option but provide direct access to your destination. 

KTX: For long-distance travel, KTX, SRT, and express buses connect Seoul with other parts of Korea. These are ideal for traveling between cities. 

For a smooth experience, it's recommended to use a transit card like T-Money, which allows for easy transfers and eliminates the need to buy tickets each time. Additionally, using navigation apps like Naver Map or KakaoMap can help you plan your route effectively.
